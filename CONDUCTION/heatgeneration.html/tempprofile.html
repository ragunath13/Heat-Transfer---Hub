For a solid generating heat internally under steady-state conditions (1D, no convection), the heat conduction equation becomes:

\[ \frac{d^2T}{dx^2} + \frac{\dot{q}}{k} = 0 \]

Where:

For a wall of half-thickness \( L \), with both surfaces held at temperature \( T_s \), the temperature distribution becomes:

\[ T(x) = T_s + \frac{\dot{q}}{2k}(L^2 - x^2) \]

This parabolic profile indicates that the temperature is highest at the center and lowest at the surfaces.
