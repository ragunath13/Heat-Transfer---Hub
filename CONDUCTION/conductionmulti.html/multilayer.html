🧱 Series Conduction Through Layered Materials

📘 Concept Overview

When heat flows through multiple solid layers placed in series (e.g., walls, insulations), the total thermal resistance is the sum of each individual layer’s resistance. This model is widely used in building insulation, furnaces, and electronic packaging.

📐 Thermal Resistance of a Single Layer

\[ R = \frac{L}{kA} \]
  • \( R \): Thermal resistance (K/W)
  • \( L \): Thickness of the material (m)
  • \( k \): Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)
  • \( A \): Cross-sectional area perpendicular to heat flow (m²)

🔗 Total Resistance in Series

For multiple layers in series, the total thermal resistance is:

\[ R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_2 + R_3 + \dots + R_n \]

Or, in terms of material properties:

\[ R_{\text{total}} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{L_i}{k_i A} \]

🔥 Heat Transfer Rate

Once total resistance is known, the heat transfer rate is given by:

\[ Q = \frac{T_{\text{hot}} - T_{\text{cold}}}{R_{\text{total}}} \]
  • \( Q \): Heat transfer rate (W)
  • \( T_{\text{hot}}, T_{\text{cold}} \): Temperatures on either side (K)

📊 Temperature Drop Across Each Layer

The temperature drop across any individual layer is:

\[ \Delta T_i = Q \cdot R_i \]

This helps in knowing how much each material contributes to thermal resistance.

🛠️ Applications

  • Insulation of building walls and roofs
  • Thermal shields in spacecraft and satellites
  • Heat exchangers with composite walls
  • Multi-layer PCBs and microchips
