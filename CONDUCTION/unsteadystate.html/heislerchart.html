🧊 What Happens Inside a Cooling Object?

Cool

When an object cools:

  • Surface cools first
  • Center stays warmer longer
  • Temperature varies through the thickness

📐 The Two Key Numbers

Biot Number (Bi)

Compares:

  • Heat transfer at surface
  • Heat conduction inside

Bi < 0.1: Uniform temperature
Bi > 0.1: Needs Heisler charts

Fourier Number (Fo)

Measures:

  • How long cooling has occurred
  • Relative to object size

Fo = 0: Just started
Fo > 1: Significant cooling

🔍 When to Use Heisler Charts

  • Biot number is greater than 0.1
  • 1D transient heat conduction dominates
  • Geometry is slab, cylinder, or sphere
  • Material properties are constant

🧠 Tips for Reading Heisler Charts Accurately

  • Use a straightedge to follow axis lines
  • Mark your Biot number curve carefully
  • Log-log or semi-log scales are common — read axes correctly!
  • Double-check your θ value on the vertical axis

📚 Quick Summary

Parameter Meaning
Biot Number (Bi) Ratio of internal to surface resistance
Fourier Number (Fo) Dimensionless time for heat diffusion
θ₀/θᵢ Temperature ratio from chart

📈 How to Use Heisler Charts

Fo
θ₀/θᵢ
  1. Calculate Bi and Fo for your problem
  2. Find curve matching your Bi value
  3. Read θ₀/θᵢ for your Fo value
  4. Multiply to get actual temperature

🏭 Real-World Example

Steel Plate:

  • 10cm thick
  • Initial: 300°C
  • Air: 25°C
  • After 5 minutes

Solution Steps:

  1. Bi = 0.011 → Lumped capacitance
  2. θ = exp(-Bi×Fo)
  3. T = 25 + (300-25)×θ
  4. Answer: ≈94°C
