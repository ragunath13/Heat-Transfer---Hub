🧊 Effect on Total Thermal Resistance

📘 Concept Overview

In thermal systems involving multiple layers of materials, **thermal resistance** plays a crucial role in determining the rate of heat transfer. Each layer contributes a conduction resistance, and the interfaces between layers introduce an additional resistance known as thermal contact resistance (Rtc).

🧮 Total Thermal Resistance Equation

The combined thermal resistance of a system is:

\[ R_{\text{total}} = R_1 + R_{tc} + R_2 \]
  • \( R_1, R_2 \): Conduction resistance of each layer
  • \( R_{tc} \): Thermal contact resistance due to imperfect surface contact

📐 Conduction Resistance of a Layer

\[ R = \frac{L}{kA} \]

Where:

  • \( L \): Thickness of the layer (m)
  • \( k \): Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)
  • \( A \): Cross-sectional area (m²)

🔍 Thermal Contact Resistance

\[ R_{tc} = \frac{1}{h_{tc} \cdot A} \]

Where:

  • \( h_{tc} \): Thermal contact conductance (W/m²·K)
  • \( A \): Contact area (m²)

⚠️ Factors Affecting Thermal Contact Resistance

  • Surface Roughness: Rougher surfaces trap air and reduce actual contact area.
  • Contact Pressure: Higher pressure improves surface conformity and decreases resistance.
  • Material Properties: Softer materials may deform better to form good contact.
  • Presence of Gaps or Voids: Trapped air is a poor conductor and increases resistance.

🛠️ Reducing Contact Resistance

  • Use of thermal interface materials (TIMs) like thermal grease, paste, or pads.
  • Polishing and machining surfaces for improved flatness.
  • Applying clamping pressure to improve contact area.
  • Coating with conductive materials like silver or copper.

🌡️ Heat Transfer Through the System

\[ Q = \frac{T_{\text{hot}} - T_{\text{cold}}}{R_{\text{total}}} \]
  • \( Q \): Heat transfer rate (W)
  • \( T_{\text{hot}}, T_{\text{cold}} \): Temperatures on either side of the system
  • \( R_{\text{total}} \): Combined thermal resistance (K/W)

📌 Engineering Significance

Thermal contact resistance is crucial in fields like:

  • Electronic device cooling (e.g., CPU and GPU heatsinks)
  • Power electronics, aerospace systems, and automotive engines
  • Thermal protection systems in spacecraft
  • Layered insulation systems

Even small changes in \( R_{tc} \) can significantly impact thermal performance and reliability.
