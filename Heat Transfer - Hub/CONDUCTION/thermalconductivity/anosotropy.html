ğŸ” What is Anisotropy?

Anisotropy means that a material has different properties in different directions. In the context of thermal conductivity, anisotropic materials conduct heat differently along different axes. This is common in composite materials, crystals, and layered structures.

ğŸ“ˆ Thermal Conductivity in Anisotropic Materials

In isotropic materials (like metals), thermal conductivity is the same in all directions. However, in anisotropic materials, conductivity depends on direction. For example:

  • In a crystal, heat may flow faster along one axis than another.
  • In a carbon fiber composite, thermal conductivity is higher along the fibers than across them.
  • Wood conducts heat better along the grain than across it.
