In cylindrical coordinates, heat conduction refers to the radial heat transfer through a cylindrical object. This is particularly relevant in situations such as the heat flow through a pipe, where thermal energy moves from the fluid inside to the insulation and then to the outer environment. The geometry of the cylindrical pipe causes the heat to flow in the radial direction.

For pipe insulation, heat transfer is governed by the material's thermal conductivity, the temperature difference between the inner and outer surfaces, and the dimensions of the pipe and insulation.
