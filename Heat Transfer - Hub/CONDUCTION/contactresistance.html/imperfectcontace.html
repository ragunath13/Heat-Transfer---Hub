🌡️ Imperfect Contact Between Surfaces

📘 Governing Concept

In heat transfer, when two surfaces are in thermal contact but imperfectly (due to surface roughness, contamination, etc.), the heat transfer between the surfaces is reduced. This phenomenon is described by the **thermal contact resistance** (\(R_{tc}\)), which represents the opposition to heat transfer at the interface.

The **heat transfer rate** (\(Q\)) across the imperfect contact interface is given by the following equation:

\[ Q = \frac{T_1 - T_2}{R_{tc}} \]

Where:

  • \(Q\): Heat transfer rate (W)
  • \(T_1\), \(T_2\): Temperatures of the two surfaces in contact (K)
  • \(R_{tc}\): Thermal contact resistance (m²·K/W)

📐 Thermal Contact Resistance

The **thermal contact resistance** \(R_{tc}\) is a measure of how much resistance to heat transfer exists at the interface between two materials. It can be approximated as:

\[ R_{tc} = \frac{1}{h_{tc} A} \]

Where:

  • \(h_{tc}\): Thermal contact conductance (W/m²·K)
  • \(A\): Contact area between the two surfaces (m²)

🌡️ Temperature Difference Across the Interface

The temperature difference across the contact interface can be calculated using the following equation:

\[ \Delta T = Q \cdot R_{tc} \]

Where:

  • \(\Delta T\): Temperature difference across the interface (K)
  • \(Q\): Heat transfer rate (W)
  • \(R_{tc}\): Thermal contact resistance (m²·K/W)

🏭 Applications

  • Heat exchangers
  • Electronic components and microprocessors
  • Engine components
  • Solar panels and their connections

📊 Example Problem

Let's consider two surfaces with the following conditions: - Temperature of surface 1: \(T_1 = 200 \, \text{K}\) - Temperature of surface 2: \(T_2 = 50 \, \text{K}\) - Contact area between the surfaces: \(A = 0.1 \, \text{m}^2\) - Thermal contact conductance: \(h_{tc} = 500 \, \text{W/m}^2 \cdot \text{K}\)

First, we calculate the **thermal contact resistance** \(R_{tc}\) as follows:

\[ R_{tc} = \frac{1}{h_{tc} A} = \frac{1}{500 \times 0.1} = 0.02 \, \text{m}^2 \cdot \text{K/W} \]

Then, the **heat transfer rate** \(Q\) across the interface is given by:

\[ Q = \frac{T_1 - T_2}{R_{tc}} = \frac{200 - 50}{0.02} = 7500 \, \text{W} \]

Thus, the heat transfer rate across the interface is \(7500 \, \text{W}\).
