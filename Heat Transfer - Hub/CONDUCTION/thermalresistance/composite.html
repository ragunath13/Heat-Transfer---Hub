🔥 Composite Wall System in Conduction

🏗️ Understanding Composite Walls

Composite walls are structures made of multiple materials with different thermal properties. They're everywhere around us:

  • Your House Walls: Drywall + insulation + brick
  • Oven Doors: Glass + air gap + metal
  • Spacecraft: Ceramic tiles + metal alloys

Why use multiple materials? Each material serves a purpose - strength, insulation, or protection!

🧩 The Three Key Concepts

To understand composite walls, you need to know these fundamentals:

1. Thermal Conductivity (k)

How easily heat flows through a material. Metals have high k, insulation has low k.

2. Thermal Resistance (R)

R = thickness/(k × area). Like electrical resistance but for heat!

3. Steady-State Heat Flow

Heat in = Heat out (like water flowing through pipes at constant rate).

📝 Step-by-Step Derivation (Made Easy!)

Step 1: The Basic Law

Fourier's Law tells us heat flow (q) depends on:

q = -k × A × (ΔT/Δx)

Where:
k = thermal conductivity
A = cross-sectional area
ΔT = temperature difference
Δx = thickness

Step 2: Define Thermal Resistance

We rearrange Fourier's Law to look like Ohm's Law (V=IR):

q = ΔT/R

Where R = Δx/(k×A) → "Thermal Resistance"

Think of R like friction against heat flow!

Step 3: For Multiple Layers

In series (one after another), resistances add up:

Rtotal = R1 + R2 + R3 + ...

So heat flow becomes:

q = (Thot - Tcold)/(R1 + R2 + ...)

Step 4: Calculate Interface Temperatures

Temperature drops at each layer (like voltage drops in circuits):

T1 = Thot - q×R1
T2 = T1 - q×R2

And so on...

Step 5: Real-World Example

Wall with brick (10cm, k=0.7) and insulation (5cm, k=0.04):

Rbrick = 0.1/(0.7×1) = 0.143 K/W
Rinsul = 0.05/(0.04×1) = 1.25 K/W
q = (20°C - (-5°C))/(0.143 + 1.25) = 17.9 W

💡 Practical Tips for Engineers

Do's

  • Put insulation on the cold side in refrigeration
  • Use thermal breaks in metal structures
  • Consider moisture resistance in materials

Don'ts

  • Don't ignore thermal bridging
  • Don't compress insulation materials
  • Don't forget expansion gaps

🔍 Advanced Concepts

For students who want to go deeper:

Parallel Heat Flow Paths

Some walls have parallel materials (like studs in a wall):

1/Rtotal = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...

Calculate each path separately then combine!

Transient (Time-Dependent) Analysis

When temperatures change over time:

∂T/∂t = α·(∂²T/∂x²)

Where α = thermal diffusivity (k/ρcp)
