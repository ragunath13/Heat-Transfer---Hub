Definition

Conduction is one of the three primary modes of heat transfer (the others being convection and radiation). It refers to the process by which heat energy is transferred through a material or between materials that are in direct physical contact, without any movement of the material as a whole. Conduction occurs due to the interaction between particles (atoms, molecules, or electrons) within a substance.

Father Of Conduction

The father of conduction is often attributed to Jean-Baptiste Joseph Fourier (1768–1830), a French mathematician and physicist. Fourier made groundbreaking contributions to the understanding of heat transfer, particularly through his work on the mathematical theory of heat conduction.
