External Flow in Forced Convection

Characteristics of External Flow

External flow occurs when a fluid flows over a body that is completely surrounded by the fluid:

  • Flow develops boundary layers along surfaces
  • Free stream velocity exists outside boundary layers
  • Important for applications like:
    • Airflow over vehicles
    • Wind over buildings
    • Cooling of electronic components
Boundary Layer Development

Key Parameters:

  • Reynolds Number: Re = ρ·v·L/μ
  • Prandtl Number: Pr = ν/α
  • Nusselt Number: Nu = h·L/k
Boundary Layer Development

Laminar Boundary Layer

  • Smooth, orderly flow near surface
  • Develops at lower Re
  • Thinner than turbulent boundary layer
  • Heat transfer primarily by conduction

Transition Region

  • Flow becomes unstable
  • Occurs at Re ≈ 2×105 for flat plates
  • Intermittent turbulent spots appear

Turbulent Boundary Layer

  • Chaotic, mixing flow near surface
  • Develops at higher Re
  • Thicker than laminar boundary layer
  • Enhanced heat transfer by eddy diffusion
Heat Transfer Correlations

For Flat Plates

Laminar Flow (Re < 5×105)

Nux = 0.332·Rex1/2·Pr1/3

Local Nusselt number at distance x from leading edge

Turbulent Flow (Re > 5×105)

Nux = 0.0296·Rex4/5·Pr1/3

Average Nusselt Number (Entire Plate)

NuL = (0.037·ReL4/5 - 871)·Pr1/3

Valid for mixed laminar and turbulent flow (Re < 108, 0.6 < Pr < 60)

Practical Applications

Aerodynamics

  • Drag reduction in vehicles
  • Boundary layer control
  • Heat transfer from aircraft surfaces

Electronic Cooling

  • Forced air cooling of components
  • Heat sink optimization
  • PCB thermal management

Building Design

  • Wind load calculations
  • HVAC system design
  • External heat transfer coefficients
Example Calculation

Air Flow Over Flat Plate

Calculate the average heat transfer coefficient for air at 20°C flowing at 10 m/s over a 1m long plate:

Properties at 20°C:

ρ = 1.204 kg/m³, μ = 1.825×10-5 Pa·s

Pr = 0.71, k = 0.0257 W/m·K

ReL = (1.204 × 10 × 1) / 1.825×10-5 ≈ 659,700

NuL = (0.037 × 659,7004/5 - 871) × 0.711/3 ≈ 753

h = Nu·k/L = 753 × 0.0257 / 1 ≈ 19.4 W/m²K
