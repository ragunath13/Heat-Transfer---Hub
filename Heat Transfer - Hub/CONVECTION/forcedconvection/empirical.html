Dittus-Boelter Equation & Empirical Correlations

Overview

The Dittus-Boelter Equation is an empirical correlation used to estimate the convective heat transfer coefficient in turbulent internal flow:

Nu = 0.023 · Re0.8 · Prn

n = 0.4 for heating (fluid is being heated)
n = 0.3 for cooling (fluid is being cooled)

Valid for:

  • Turbulent flow: Re > 10,000
  • Moderate Prandtl numbers: 0.7 < Pr < 160
  • Uniform wall heat flux or temperature
Comparison with Other Correlations

Dittus-Boelter

  • Simple and widely used
  • Good for smooth pipes
  • Valid for Re > 10⁴

Sieder-Tate

  • Includes viscosity variation
  • More accurate for variable properties
  • Nu = 0.027 · Re0.8 · Pr1/3 · (μ/μw)0.14

Gnielinski

  • Applicable to broader ranges
  • Based on f friction factor
  • Accurate but more complex
Example Calculation

Water Heated in a Pipe

Hot water flows at 2 m/s through a pipe of diameter 0.02 m. Properties at bulk temp:

ρ = 998 kg/m³, μ = 0.001 Pa·s

Pr = 5.8, k = 0.6 W/m·K

Re = (998 × 2 × 0.02) / 0.001 = 39,920

Nu = 0.023 × (39920)0.8 × (5.8)0.4 ≈ 144.2

h = Nu·k/D = 144.2 × 0.6 / 0.02 = 4326 W/m²·K

Dittus-Boelter Equation

The Dittus-Boelter equation is an empirical correlation used to estimate the convective heat transfer coefficient in internal flow for fully developed turbulent conditions.

General Form:

Nu = 0.023 · Re0.8 · Prn

  • Use n = 0.4 for heating the fluid
  • Use n = 0.3 for cooling the fluid

Assumptions and Applicability:

  • Re > 10,000 (Turbulent flow)
  • 0.7 ≤ Pr ≤ 160
  • Constant wall heat flux or wall temperature
  • Fluid properties are evaluated at bulk mean temperature
  • Smooth circular tubes

Engineering Insight:

The Dittus-Boelter equation is widely used due to its simplicity and reasonable accuracy for many engineering applications such as:

  • Cooling of engine components with oil or water
  • Air conditioning and HVAC system design
  • Boiler and heat exchanger analysis

Example Calculation

Water at 60°C flows through a pipe (D = 0.02 m) at 3 m/s. Estimate the heat transfer coefficient.

Properties: ρ = 983 kg/m³, μ = 4.67×10-4 Pa·s, Pr = 3.98, k = 0.653 W/m·K

Re = (983 × 3 × 0.02) / 4.67×10-4 ≈ 126,112

Nu = 0.023 × (126112)0.8 × (3.98)0.4 ≈ 241.8

h = Nu·k/D = 241.8 × 0.653 / 0.02 ≈ 7,892 W/m²K
