Dominance Criterion: Gr/Re² in Convection

Understanding how the ratio of buoyancy to inertial forces determines convection regimes

Natural Convection Dominant (Gr/Re² > 10)

Characteristics:

  • Buoyancy forces dominate flow
  • Velocity fields driven by temperature gradients
  • Boundary layers develop vertically
  • Heat transfer correlations use Grashof number
Natural Dominance

Forced Convection Dominant (Gr/Re² < 0.1)

Characteristics:

  • Inertial forces dominate flow
  • Temperature fields adapt to velocity fields
  • Boundary layers develop along flow direction
  • Heat transfer correlations use Reynolds number
Forced Dominance

Mixed Convection (0.1 < Gr/Re² < 10)

Characteristics:

  • Buoyancy and inertial forces comparable
  • Complex interaction between flow fields
  • May exhibit flow reversal or instability
  • Requires combined correlations
Mixed Regime

Key Concept: Richardson Number (Ri = Gr/Re²)

The Richardson number quantifies the relative importance of natural to forced convection:

Ri = Gr/Re² = (Buoyancy Force)/(Inertial Force) = (g·β·ΔT·L)/V²

Where:
g = gravitational acceleration
β = thermal expansion coefficient
ΔT = temperature difference
L = characteristic length
V = flow velocity

Regime Classification by Ri
Regime Ri Range Dominant Physics Heat Transfer Correlation
Pure Forced Ri < 0.1 Inertial forces dominate Nu = f(Re, Pr)
Mixed - Forced Dominant 0.1 < Ri < 1 Forced flow with buoyancy effects Nu = Nuforced[1 + aRib]
Mixed - Natural Dominant 1 < Ri < 10 Natural convection with flow effects Nu = Nunatural[1 + cRi-d]
Pure Natural Ri > 10 Buoyancy forces dominate Nu = f(Gr, Pr)
Practical Implications

Forced-Dominant Systems

  • High velocity gas flows (HVAC ducts)
  • Pumped liquid cooling systems
  • External flows over surfaces
  • Ri typically 0.001-0.01

Mixed Convection Systems

  • Low velocity ventilation
  • Solar chimney flows
  • Electronics cooling
  • Ri typically 0.5-5

Natural-Dominant Systems

  • Passive building ventilation
  • Heat sink natural convection
  • Geothermal flows
  • Ri typically 10-1000
Calculation Examples

Forced-Dominant Example

Airflow over heated plate:

V = 5 m/s, L = 0.1m, ΔT = 20K
Gr ≈ 1×106, Re ≈ 3×104
Ri = Gr/Re² ≈ 0.001

Clearly forced-dominant regime

Natural-Dominant Example

Vertical heated plate:

V ≈ 0.1 m/s, L = 0.5m, ΔT = 30K
Gr ≈ 3×107, Re ≈ 3×103
Ri = Gr/Re² ≈ 30

Clearly natural-dominant regime

Design Considerations

When evaluating convection systems:

  1. Calculate both Gr and Re numbers
  2. Determine Ri = Gr/Re²
  3. Select appropriate correlation based on Ri range
  4. For mixed convection (0.1 < Ri < 10), use combined correlations
The Ri number provides a clear criterion for determining when natural convection effects become significant in forced flow systems.
