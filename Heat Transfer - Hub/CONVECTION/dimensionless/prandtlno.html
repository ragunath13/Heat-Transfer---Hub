Prandtl Number in Heat Transfer

What is the Prandtl Number?

The Prandtl Number (Pr) is a dimensionless quantity that relates momentum diffusivity to thermal diffusivity:

Pr = ν/α = μ·cp/k

ν: Kinematic viscosity (m²/s) α: Thermal diffusivity (m²/s) μ: Dynamic viscosity (Pa·s) cp: Specific heat (J/kg·K) k: Thermal conductivity (W/m·K)

Physical Interpretation:

  • Compares how quickly momentum diffuses relative to heat
  • Determines the relative thickness of velocity and thermal boundary layers
  • Key parameter in convective heat transfer correlations
Prandtl Number Spectrum
Liquid Metals
Pr ≈ 0.01-0.1
Gases
Pr ≈ 0.7-1.0
Water
Pr ≈ 1-10
Oils
Pr ≈ 100-1000

Pr < 1 (Liquid Metals)

  • Heat diffuses faster than momentum
  • Thermal boundary layer thicker than velocity boundary layer
  • Excellent heat transfer properties
  • Example: Mercury (Pr ≈ 0.025)

Pr ≈ 1 (Gases)

  • Heat and momentum diffuse at similar rates
  • Boundary layers of similar thickness
  • Example: Air (Pr ≈ 0.71)

Pr > 1 (Oils)

  • Momentum diffuses faster than heat
  • Velocity boundary layer thicker than thermal boundary layer
  • Poor heat transfer properties
  • Example: Engine oil (Pr ≈ 100-1000)
Role in Heat Transfer Calculations

Prandtl Number in Convective Heat Transfer

The Prandtl Number appears in all convective heat transfer correlations:

Nu = C·Rem·Prn

Where Nu is the Nusselt number and Re is the Reynolds number.

Practical Implications:

  • For Pr < 1, heat transfer is more efficient than momentum transfer
  • For Pr > 1, momentum transfers more readily than heat
  • Affects the design of heat exchangers and cooling systems

Thermal vs Momentum Boundary Layers

Prandtl Number determines their relative thickness

Example Calculations

Calculating Prandtl Number for Water at 20°C

μ = 0.001002 Pa·s

cp = 4182 J/kg·K

k = 0.603 W/m·K

Pr = (0.001002 × 4182) / 0.603 ≈ 6.99

This Pr ≈ 7 indicates that for water, momentum diffuses about 7 times faster than heat.
