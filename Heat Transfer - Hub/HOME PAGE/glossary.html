Heat Transfer Glossary

Key terms and definitions in the field of heat transfer

Conduction

Heat transfer through direct physical contact, where thermal energy is transferred from more energetic particles to less energetic ones.

Convection

Heat transfer through the movement of fluids (liquids or gases). It can be natural (due to density differences) or forced (due to external means like a fan).

Radiation

Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves, which does not require a medium. All objects with temperature above absolute zero emit thermal radiation.

Thermal Conductivity (k)

A material's ability to conduct heat. It quantifies how readily heat flows through a material under a temperature gradient.

Convection Coefficient (h)

A proportionality constant that relates the heat flux to the temperature difference between a surface and a fluid. Also known as film coefficient.

Emissivity (ε)

A measure of a material's ability to emit thermal radiation, relative to that of a perfect blackbody. Ranges from 0 to 1.

Blackbody

An idealized physical body that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation, regardless of frequency or angle of incidence. It is also a perfect emitter of thermal radiation.

Stefan-Boltzmann Law

Describes the power radiated from a blackbody in terms of its temperature. The total energy radiated per unit surface area of a black body across all wavelengths per unit time is directly proportional to the fourth power of the black body's absolute temperature.

Thermal Resistance (R)

A measure of a material's or object's opposition to the flow of heat. It is the reciprocal of thermal conductance.

Heat Flux (q)

The rate of heat energy transfer per unit area. It is a vector quantity, indicating both the magnitude and direction of heat flow.

Overall Heat Transfer Coefficient (U)

A measure of the overall rate of heat transfer through a system, accounting for all modes of heat transfer and resistances between two fluids or a fluid and a solid.

Fourier's Law

States that the rate of heat conduction through a material is proportional to the negative gradient in the temperature and to the area perpendicular to that gradient.

Nusselt Number (Nu)

A dimensionless number that measures the ratio of convective to conductive heat transfer across a boundary. It indicates the enhancement of heat transfer due to convection.

Prandtl Number (Pr)

A dimensionless number that relates the momentum diffusivity to the thermal diffusivity. It is used to characterize the relative thickness of the velocity and thermal boundary layers.

Reynolds Number (Re)

A dimensionless number that predicts flow patterns in different fluid flow situations. It is the ratio of inertial forces to viscous forces.

Thermal Diffusivity (α)

A measure of how quickly temperature changes propagate through a material. It is the ratio of thermal conductivity to the product of density and specific heat capacity.

Latent Heat

The heat absorbed or released during a phase change (e.g., melting, boiling) at constant temperature and pressure, without a change in temperature.

Specific Heat Capacity (c_p)

The amount of heat energy required to raise the temperature of a unit mass of a substance by one degree Celsius (or Kelvin) at constant pressure.

Heat Exchanger

A device built for efficient heat transfer from one fluid to another, whether the fluids are separated by a solid wall or in direct contact.

Fin Efficiency

A measure of how effectively a fin transfers heat compared to an ideal fin of infinite thermal conductivity. It indicates the performance of extended surfaces.

Biot Number (Bi)

A dimensionless number used in transient heat conduction that relates the heat transfer resistance at the surface of a body to the heat conduction resistance within the body.

Lumped Capacitance Method

A simplification in transient heat conduction analysis where the temperature within an object is assumed to be spatially uniform at any instant in time.

Log Mean Temperature Difference (LMTD)

A method used to calculate the average temperature difference between two fluids in a heat exchanger, especially when temperatures vary along the flow path.

Effectiveness-NTU Method

An alternative method to LMTD for heat exchanger analysis, particularly useful when inlet or outlet temperatures are unknown.

Fouling Factor (R_f)

A measure of the thermal resistance caused by the accumulation of deposits (fouling) on the heat transfer surfaces of heat exchangers, reducing efficiency.

Critical Thickness of Insulation

The thickness of insulation at which the heat transfer rate from a pipe or sphere is maximized. Beyond this thickness, adding more insulation actually reduces heat transfer.

Thermal Boundary Layer

The region of a fluid flow over a surface where the temperature gradients are significant and the fluid temperature changes from the surface temperature to the free stream temperature.

View Factor (F_ij)

A geometric parameter that describes the fraction of radiation leaving surface 'i' that is intercepted by surface 'j'. Also known as shape factor or configuration factor.

Absorptivity (α)

The fraction of incident thermal radiation that is absorbed by a surface. For a blackbody, absorptivity is 1.

Reflectivity (ρ)

The fraction of incident thermal radiation that is reflected by a surface. For an opaque surface, $\alpha + \rho = 1$.

Transmissivity (τ)

The fraction of incident thermal radiation that passes through a material. For a semi-transparent medium, $\alpha + \rho + \tau = 1$.

Gray Body

A body whose emissivity (and thus absorptivity) is independent of wavelength. It's a simplification used in radiation heat transfer.

Shape Factor (S)

A geometric parameter used in conduction heat transfer for complex geometries, relating the heat transfer rate to thermal conductivity and temperature difference.

Thermal Contact Resistance

The resistance to heat flow across the interface between two solid materials due to imperfect contact (e.g., surface roughness, air gaps).

Heat Generation

The production of thermal energy within a medium, such as from electrical resistance, chemical reactions, or nuclear fission.

Steady-State Heat Transfer

A condition where the temperature distribution within a system does not change with time. Heat input equals heat output.

Transient Heat Transfer

A condition where the temperature distribution within a system changes with time. Heat input does not equal heat output.

Convective Heat Transfer Coefficient (h)

The proportionality constant between the heat flux and the thermodynamic driving force for the flow of heat (i.e., the temperature difference).

Forced Convection

Heat transfer that occurs when fluid motion is induced by external means, such as a pump, fan, or stirrer.

Natural (Free) Convection

Heat transfer that occurs due to buoyancy forces arising from density differences caused by temperature variations in the fluid.

Boiling

A phase change process where a liquid rapidly vaporizes, typically occurring when the liquid's temperature reaches its saturation temperature at a given pressure.

Condensation

A phase change process where a vapor changes into a liquid, typically occurring when the vapor's temperature drops below its saturation temperature.

Film Condensation

A type of condensation where the condensate forms a continuous liquid film that covers the entire condensing surface.

Dropwise Condensation

A type of condensation where the condensate forms discrete droplets on the condensing surface, leading to much higher heat transfer coefficients than film condensation.

Nucleate Boiling

A regime of boiling characterized by the formation of vapor bubbles at discrete nucleation sites on the heated surface.

Film Boiling

A regime of boiling where the heated surface is completely covered by a continuous vapor film, leading to a significant reduction in heat transfer due to the insulating effect of the vapor.

Heat Pipe

A heat transfer device that uses phase transitions (evaporation and condensation) of a working fluid to transfer large amounts of heat with a very small temperature difference.

Heat Pump

A device that transfers thermal energy from a source of heat to a "sink" of heat against a temperature gradient, often used for heating or cooling buildings.

Regenerator

A type of heat exchanger where heat is alternately stored and released by a solid matrix, typically used to recover heat from exhaust gases.

Recuperator

A type of heat exchanger where heat is continuously transferred between two fluid streams separated by a solid wall, without intermediate heat storage.
