Definition

Radiation is one of the three primary modes of heat transfer (the others being conduction and convection). It involves the transfer of heat through electromagnetic waves, without the need for any medium or direct contact between objects. All bodies emit thermal radiation depending on their temperature, and this energy can travel through a vacuum, such as from the Sun to Earth.

Father Of Radiation

Max Planck is considered a key figure in the study of thermal radiation. His work on blackbody radiation led to the development of quantum theory, fundamentally changing our understanding of energy emission and the nature of radiation.
