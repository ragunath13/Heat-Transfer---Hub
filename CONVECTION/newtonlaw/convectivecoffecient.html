Convective Heat Transfer

Fundamental Equation

q = h · A · (Ts - T∞)
q: Heat transfer rate (W) h: Convective coefficient (W/m²K) Ts: Surface temperature (K) T∞: Fluid temperature (K)

The convective heat transfer coefficient (h) quantifies how effectively heat transfers between a surface and moving fluid.

Dimensionless Numbers

Nusselt Number

Nu = hL/k

Ratio of convective to conductive heat transfer

Prandtl Number

Pr = ν/α = μcp/k

Ratio of momentum to thermal diffusivity

Reynolds Number

Re = ρvL/μ

Ratio of inertial to viscous forces

Grashof Number

Gr = gβΔTL³/ν²

Ratio of buoyancy to viscous forces

Heat Transfer Correlations

Natural Convection

Nu = C(Gr · Pr)n

Where C and n depend on geometry and flow regime

Forced Convection

Nu = C Rem Prn

Where C, m, n depend on geometry and flow conditions

Practical Values of h

Natural Convection

  • Air: 5-25 W/m²K
  • Water: 50-1000 W/m²K

Forced Convection

  • Air: 25-250 W/m²K
  • Water: 100-15000 W/m²K

Boiling

  • Water: 2500-100000 W/m²K

Condensation

  • Steam: 4000-100000 W/m²K
Physical Interpretation
h = 100 W/m²K

Means: For each 1 m² of surface area and 1K temperature difference,
100 Joules of heat transfer occurs every second
