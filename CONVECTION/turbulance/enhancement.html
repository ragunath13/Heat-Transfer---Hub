Enhancement of Heat Transfer

Definition: Enhancement of heat transfer refers to methods designed to increase the thermal energy exchange between a surface and fluid. These techniques are essential for improving the performance and energy efficiency of systems such as heat exchangers, electronic cooling, and solar collectors.

In many modern applications, space constraints and performance requirements make heat transfer enhancement not just beneficial but necessary.

Types of Enhancement Techniques
1. Passive Techniques:

These methods enhance heat transfer without any external power input. Examples include:

  • Extended Surfaces (Fins): Increase surface area for better heat dissipation.
  • Twisted Tapes: Induce swirl flow to increase turbulence.
  • Surface Roughness: Interrupts boundary layer to promote mixing.
  • Porous Media: Promotes enhanced conduction and mixing.
2. Active Techniques:

Require external energy to enhance heat transfer:

  • Electromagnetic fields to influence electrically conductive fluids.
  • Surface vibration to disrupt thermal boundary layers.
  • Fluid injection/suction to promote mixing at boundaries.
3. Compound Techniques:

Combine passive and active methods for superior performance. For example, using vibrating fins in combination with twisted tape inserts.

Why Enhance Heat Transfer?
  • Boost overall thermal efficiency
  • Reduce system size and weight
  • Lower operating costs
  • Improve heat exchanger performance
  • Achieve more compact and sustainable designs
Common Applications
  • Electronics cooling (e.g., CPU/GPU heat sinks)
  • Automotive systems: radiators, intercoolers
  • HVAC units and heat pumps
  • Energy storage systems (batteries, fuel cells)
  • Compact heat exchangers in aerospace
Sample Correlation

For turbulent flow with enhancement techniques (like twisted tape inserts), the Nusselt number may be given by:

Nu = 0.023 Re^0.8 Pr^0.4 (1 + C ξ)

Where:

  • Re: Reynolds number
  • Pr: Prandtl number
  • ξ: Enhancement factor based on geometry
  • C: Empirical constant
Recent Advances
Nanofluids:

Suspending nanoparticles (e.g., Al₂O₃, CuO) in base fluids like water significantly improves thermal conductivity and convective heat transfer.

Additive Manufacturing:

3D printing enables complex fin and channel designs previously impossible with traditional methods, achieving ultra-compact high-efficiency exchangers.

Phase Change Materials (PCMs):

Used for latent heat storage and thermal buffering in electronics, solar thermal, and battery applications.
