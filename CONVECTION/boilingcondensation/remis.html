Boiling Regimes: Nucleate, Transition, Film

Boiling occurs in distinct regimes as heat flux increases, each with unique characteristics and heat transfer mechanisms.

Three Primary Boiling Regimes
NUCLEATE BOILING
  • Individual bubbles form at nucleation sites
  • High heat transfer coefficients
  • Bubbles detach and rise through liquid
  • Optimal boiling regime
TRANSITION BOILING
  • Unstable mixture of nucleate and film boiling
  • Partial vapor film coverage
  • Heat flux decreases with ΔT increase
  • Difficult to maintain steady-state
FILM BOILING
  • Continuous vapor film blankets surface
  • Radiation becomes significant
  • Higher ΔT but lower heat transfer
  • Leidenfrost effect observed

Key Concepts

  • Critical Heat Flux (CHF): Peak heat flux in nucleate boiling before transition to film boiling
  • Leidenfrost Point: Minimum heat flux in transition boiling
  • Hysteresis: Different paths when increasing vs decreasing heat flux
DID YOU KNOW? The critical heat flux for water at 1 atm is ~1 MW/m²!
