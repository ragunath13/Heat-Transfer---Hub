Condensation Regimes: Filmwise and Dropwise

Condensation occurs when vapor loses heat energy and transitions to liquid, forming either continuous films or discrete droplets based on surface conditions.

Two Primary Condensation Regimes
DROPVISE CONDENSATION
  • Discrete droplets form on the surface
  • 10-20x higher heat transfer than filmwise
  • Requires non-wettable surfaces or coatings
  • Droplets grow and merge until falling/rolling
  • Common in steam power plants with promoted surfaces
FILMWISE CONDENSATION
  • Continuous liquid film covers the surface
  • Lower heat transfer due to thermal resistance
  • Occurs on clean, wettable surfaces
  • Gravity-driven film flow
  • More stable and predictable than dropwise
Key Differences

Industrial Applications
  • Power Generation: Dropwise condensation improves steam turbine efficiency by 5-10%
  • HVAC Systems: Filmwise condensation dominates in air conditioning condensers
  • Desalination: Hybrid surfaces promote dropwise condensation in multi-effect distillation
  • Electronics Cooling: Micro/nano-engineered surfaces enhance dropwise condensation
  • Aerospace: Special coatings prevent ice formation through controlled condensation
